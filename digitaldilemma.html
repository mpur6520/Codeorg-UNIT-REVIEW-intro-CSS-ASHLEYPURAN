DIGITAL DILEMMA ARTICLE 1
WHAT WHY
Video game designers are recreating athletes' tattoos in their games--however, the athletes don't inherently own the tattoos. The tattoo artists do, under copyright. These athletes' tattoos are recreated to better fit the identity of the person and how they exhibit themselves.
BENEFITS & HARM BETTER OR WORSE

HARM

  • Some artists want to sue for this, as it's technically their art.

BENEFIT

  • It allows for the characters to be a depiction of the athlete's personality, and fit their persona and identity.
  • It's seen as personal freedom for someone to express and license their image.
 I'd argue that this is for the better, on some circumstances--if the athlete is able to sign an agreement with the tattoo artist saying that it's alright, then I feel like it's for the better. I also think it depends on how clear the tattoo is, because if it's barely legible then I don't think it's as big of a problem--Also, I think adding the fact that it's the athlete showcasing the tattoo and having it as a part of their identity makes the situation a bit tentative




DIGITAL DILEMMA ARTICLE 2
WHAT WHY
People have begun to digitalize DNA as well as promote online DNA testing. They store DNA in an online database, which can be detrimental to biosecurity. When DNA is stored in an online database, there's easy access to it; people can use it for many things within the criminal and medical field, but that doesn't mean it's safe.
BENEFITS & HARM BETTER OR WORSE

HARM

  • Online DNA tests can be easily hacked into, posing a security risk.
  • It can be used to create biological weapons.
  • People can now fabricate DNA evidence and construct it.
  • It may be hacked and used for ransom or extortion.

BENEFIT

  • This allows police to identify people quickly.
  • It can assist in finding cures for diseases.
 I'd argue that this is for the worse. The harmful factors outweigh the positive, and as described in the article, this sort of stuff carries massive security issues. Especially in the case of online DNA testing services, the risk isn't worth it--you can go to a more secure place in real life, where there's less risk of confidential information being spilled out for people to see.




DIGITAL DILEMMA ARTICLE 3
WHAT WHY
People are using CGI to essentially "revive" actors who are dead; they use technology to replicate them inside of movies, which causes ethical problems. In cases such as reoccurring characters or those who experienced an untimely death during the middle of films, CGI, or computer-generated imagery, was chosen to recreate those people to play as the characters.
BENEFITS & HARM BETTER OR WORSE

HARM

  • To some people, it's unnerving to see a dead person as if they were alive.
  • Some may not want their acting career to feature something they essentially had nothing to do with.
  • Actors are unable to give their consent for this to happen.

BENEFIT

  • Films are able to bring back characters that their audience likes.
  • It can be used to honor someone's legacy, who worked hard on something such as a movie but was unable to finish.
 I'd argue that this is for the worst, but could be better--if there was a system, such as a contract, that allows actors to consent for this before they're dead, then it would be more for the better. Ethical wise I'd say it's for the worse, but production wise I'd say it's for the better. This entirely depends on whether or not you care about ethics in the first place too, because it's a topic that can be different for everyone; I'd say the situation is tentative.
